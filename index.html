The Efficiency Gap: Engine vs. Motor

Even when both ultimately rely on oil (a worst-case for EVs), electric motors are fundamentally more efficient at converting energy into motion than internal combustion engines. Here's why:

1. Internal Combustion Engine (ICE) Pathway (Oil → Fuel → Wheels)

  • **Refining crude to petrol/diesel:** ~85-90% efficient.
  • **Engine efficiency (fuel in tank → wheels):** ~20-30% (most energy lost as heat).
  • **Total efficiency from barrel to wheels:** Roughly 17-25%.

2. Electric Vehicle (EV) Pathway (Oil → Electricity → Battery → Wheels)

(Assuming oil is burned in a power plant to generate electricity)

  • **Burning oil in a power plant (oil → electricity):** ~35-45% efficient (much higher for modern plants than small engines).
  • **Electricity transmission & distribution:** ~90-95% efficient.
  • **Charging + battery losses:** ~85-90% efficient.
  • **Electric motor + drivetrain (battery → wheels):** ~85-95% efficient (vastly more efficient than ICE).
  • **Total efficiency from barrel to wheels:** Roughly 25-35%.

This shows that even when electricity comes from burning oil, the EV pathway delivers more usable energy. In reality, most grids (like NZ's) use significant renewable energy, making the EV's advantage even larger.
